Paira: making networking feel less transactional
and more human for Gen-Z.

As founding product designer, I helped shape Paira from early product framing into a warmer, clearer networking experience grounded in trust, ease, and meaningful connection.

Role

Founding Product Designer (Team size: 6)

Scope

0→1 product direction, UX, interaction, brand expression

Timeline

Aug 2025–Present

Stage

Early startup, case study shared at a high level

Overview

A networking product for people tired of performative networking.

Paira started with a simple tension: younger users know networking matters, but the experience often feels awkward, extractive, and hard to trust. Most platforms optimize for reach, not resonance.

The opportunity was to design a product that felt emotionally safer and more intentional, while still helping users discover the right people, start conversations, and build real momentum.

What I Led

Product framing

Helped define the early experience strategy, core interaction patterns, and the emotional tone Paira should hold from first impression to follow-up.

Interaction design

Explored onboarding, profile expression, discovery, and lightweight connection flows that reduce friction without making the product feel generic.

Visual language

Shaped a warmer, more conversational interface direction so the product felt welcoming rather than overly polished or corporate.

Design Principles

01

Lower the social cost

Make starting a conversation feel lighter, clearer, and less intimidating.

02

Design for sincerity

Favor cues that signal personality, context, and mutual relevance over pure polish.

03

Keep momentum visible

Help users understand where they are in the process of meeting, connecting, and following up.
